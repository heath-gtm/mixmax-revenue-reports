Channel-by-Channel Strategy
Inbound — Rebuild MQL Volume

Q1 Inbound MQLs landed at 56% of target. Heath's stated Q2 focus is measuring whether this recovers. Target for Q2: return Inbound MQL run-rate to ≥90% of monthly target by end of April.

  • Joint SDR + Marketing review of MQL sources (April 20). Identify top 2 MQL source degradations.
  • Weekly Inbound MQL scorecard in the GTM Weekly starting Week 3 of April.
  • Inbound Pipeline Q2 target: $746K (vs $126K Q1 actual).
Outbound — Root-Cause Fix

Q1 Outbound ran 5% Accounts-to-Meetings conversion vs ~18% historical — the "collapse" that drove the pipeline gap. Q2 target demands 56x the Q1 output; this won't come from incremental effort, it requires a rebuild.

  • April 20: Outbound root-cause diagnostic memo (targeting, sequencing, messaging, rep concentration).
  • April 25: Account-by-account review of the 315 Q1 Outbound accounts engaged. What % were multi-threaded?
  • April–May: +600 account engagement push to close Q2 coverage gap.
  • Weekly Outbound scorecard in GTM Weekly. If diagnostic doesn't produce a credible plan by April 25, re-baseline Outbound target with CRO.
Product — Hold the Line, Measure Continuity

Q1 Product over-performed (832% PQLs, 450% SQOs, 100% SQL→SQO). Q2 target is flat-to-Q1 on pipeline ($74K), which is the right call — we don't yet know if Q1 was a one-quarter aberration or a sustainable signal. Q2 is the measurement quarter.

  • Track weekly: PQL volume, PQL→SQO conversion, PQL-originated ARR.
  • Q2 mid-point review (~May 15): if Product volume holds above 75% of Q1 rate, recommend Product investment shift in H2.
Expansion — Replicate the Tegus Playbook

DS Expansion Contribution delivered 180% of plan in Q1 ($428K vs $238K target) on the strength of Tegus (+$49,800 / +71% at renewal under Edwin Chun). Q2 strategy is to codify and replicate.

  • April 30: Tegus playbook documented as the template for retention-to-expansion.
  • Every CSM identifies 2 accounts in their Q2 book with Tegus-like patterns (expanding usage, multi-team adoption, upcoming renewal).
CS / Renewals — Stop the Bleeding

Q1 NRR landed at 56%; GRR at 53%. Q2 targets are 85% and 80% — a 27–29 pt jump. The lift comes from (1) book rebalance on Heather Mae's portfolio, (2) codified save plays for preventable downgrades, (3) ICP-fit win-back on Q1 churn.
