Q1 2026 saw 12 deals close for $86,517 ARR (11 Net New + 1 Expansion). The dominant theme was Boomerang (returning) customers at 41.2% of ARR, with Inbound driving 62% of channel mix. However, a critical counter-signal: 49% of closed ARR ($42K) is showing at-risk adoption signals post-close, primarily from accounts that scored Low/Low on Aero fit. The two largest deals — Chicory ($16K) and Get Covered ($15K) — have both gone silent in Amplitude. Morgan Wible closed 9 of 12 deals (63% of ARR), demonstrating consistent execution across channels. The report's central finding: winning on "Ease of Use / Previous Customer" does not guarantee adoption — boomerang muscle memory closes deals but does not reliably produce post-close engagement without structured onboarding.

Key Takeaways

Boomerang dominates at 41.2% of ARR — four returning customers (Chicory, Agile Cloud, Landmark Global, Enformion) drove $35.7K. The product's "muscle memory" in former customer orgs is a real acquisition lever, but it doesn't guarantee adoption: Chicory and Enformion are both declining post-close.

Speed to Value (27%) delivered the healthiest cohort — 2-day closes (MagicLinks, Small Batch) and fast 19-day closes (CivilGrid) correlate with Stable/Ramping post-close adoption. Urgency at close-time seems to translate into urgency to adopt.

Price/Value (26%) is the riskiest theme — all 3 Price/Value wins (Get Covered, Great Point Partners, nirvanatech) have weak-to-no ICP fit. Two of three are already declining in Amplitude. Winning on price without fit is not winning.

Product Fit is tiny (6%) and represents a nonprofit outlier — SAFE is not a commercial GTM win; it's a mission-driven org that ran a 5-month trial. This theme's absence in Q1 is notable.

Winning Playbook — Keep / Stop / Start

KEEP

  • Boomerang motion is the #1 win engine. 41% of ARR came from returning customers. Product loyalty and muscle memory are real — keep nurturing former customer relationships.
  • Inbound is dominant. 62% of ARR from Inbound. The brand and product reputation are generating pipeline.
  • Speed to Value plays. 2-day closes (MagicLinks, Small Batch) show that urgent need + fast onboarding = signed deals.
  • Morgan Wible's volume. 9 of 12 deals (63% of ARR). Consistent execution across channels.

STOP

  • Closing non-ICP accounts without adoption plans. 5 of 12 deals (49% of ARR) are at risk post-close. Need qualification criteria or mandatory onboarding programs.
  • Single-threading deals. Enformion (1 user active) was single-threaded with the champion only — no team deployment ever happened.
  • Closing without recording meetings. Get Covered ($15K) and Great Point Partners ($7K) both closed with zero meetings logged. No intelligence for CSM, no relationship map for renewal.

START

  • Mandatory onboarding for deals > $5K. WeWork India ($5.9K, zero usage), Get Covered ($15K, went silent), Chicory ($16K, went silent) all needed structured onboarding.
  • Post-close health checks at 30/60/90 days. Early intervention could save the $42K at-risk ARR.
  • ICP scoring integration. Aero Low/Low accounted for 47% of ARR. Either recalibrate Aero or use it to trigger mandatory onboarding for off-ICP wins.
  • Expand Isabelle + Karan. Both closed the largest deals ($16K, $15K). Give them more at-bats.
  • Boomerang outreach program. Systematically re-engage churned accounts 1–2 quarters after churn. Agile Cloud & Landmark Global prove the play.
