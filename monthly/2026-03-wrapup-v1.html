TL;DR — What Happened

March closed with Total ARR at $8.50M against an $8.94M target — a $440K (5%) gap. Direct Sales came in 9% under plan at $4.54M while Self-Serve held steady at $3.96M, slightly above its $3.93M target. The month's story is DS Churn: we lost $273K against an $85K budget, more than tripling the planned impact and single-handedly driving the ARR miss. Total bookings landed at $30K of a $112K target (27%). On the positive side, SS Reactivation hit 149% of target and Downgrade came in better than budget at 151%.

Wins — What's Working

  • SS Reactivation at 149% of target ($34.8K vs $23.4K) — organic re-engagement driving meaningful ARR recovery
  • DS Downgrade came in better than budget at 151% (-$39.0K vs -$80.1K budget) — fewer customers stepping down plans than expected
  • PQLs at 656% of target (59 vs 9) — the product-led motion is generating demand at scale
  • Product pipeline at 549% of target ($58.0K vs $10.6K) — PLG engine converting interest to qualified pipeline
  • SS Upgrade at 116% of target ($29.4K vs $25.3K) — existing customers expanding into higher tiers
  • Expansion ARR Contribution at $60.8K (73% of target) — healthy cross-sell motion from existing DS accounts

Losses — What's Not Working

  • DS Churn at -$273K vs -$85K budget (3.2x over) — single biggest factor in the ARR miss, overwhelming all other contributions
  • Outbound pipeline at 5% of target ($8.4K vs $157.5K) — the channel is effectively offline, generating almost no new pipeline
  • Total bookings at 27% of target ($30.3K vs $112.1K) — deal velocity across all channels far too slow
  • CSM-owned GRR(ARR) at 21% — managed accounts churning faster than unmanaged VSB accounts (139% GRR)
  • Accounts Engaged at 15% of target (182 vs 1,180) — prospecting volume critically low, especially Outbound at 10%
