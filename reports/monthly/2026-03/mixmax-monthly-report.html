Executive Summary

March closed $260K under Total ARR target ($8.68M vs $8.94M, −2.9%), driven entirely by Direct Sales. The month's headline is a top-of-funnel collapse: total bookings landed at 27% of target ($30K of $112K), New Business at 29%, and Outbound pipeline creation came in at 5% of target. What saved March's Net ARR number was retention: Churn and Downgrade both finished under budget, and Expansion and Self-Serve Reactivation over-delivered — so Net ARR Contribution beat target ($18K vs $6K, +301%). Keep in mind these are two different clocks: March ARR reflects subscription activity that already landed, while March bookings mostly won't show up in ARR until April or later. The $82K bookings miss is a forward-looking problem — it hits the April/May ARR lines, not March's. That's why the picture going into April is a pipeline-gap problem first and a New Business problem second. Product-channel PQLs and meetings are outperforming — that's the one bright spot we want to protect while we fix Outbound.

Timing note — Bookings vs. ARR: Bookings (deals closed in a month) and recognized ARR (subscription revenue live in a month) do not share the same month. Most bookings signed in month M start billing in M+1 or later. So a bookings miss in March is an ARR risk for April–May, not a hit to March's ARR line. Read the Revenue Position table as a retrospective on prior-period bookings; read the Bookings & Pipeline section as the leading indicator for the next 30–60 days.

Team Attack Plan — April

AE Team

  • Re-qualify the 93-deal AE forecast: which Omitted deals can credibly move to Pipeline or Best Case in April?
  • Spend the first two weeks of April on creation, not just progression. Every AE surfaces 5 net-new ICP prospects from the Cold list for SDR/AE partnering.
  • Diagnose the Product→close handoff. 58 PQLs and 15 meetings produced $0 in DS bookings — that's a leak that needs a root cause, not just more volume.

SDR Team

  • Outbound needs a reset, not more activity on the same list. Use Aero Blended ≥ 80 on Cold ICP accounts to build a fresh target list for April (141 such accounts in the snapshot).
  • Rebuild the Accounts Engaged target with RevOps — 945 is either a capacity number or a stretch; if it's stretch, we need an interim plan that's credible against April team capacity.
  • Inbound MQL → SQL conversion was 35% in March; Product → SQL was 40%. Replicate Product's qualifying pattern on Inbound.

CS Team

  • Complete save-plan reviews on all 9 at-risk accounts in April. Prioritize the 7 VSB at-risk — that's the concentration.
  • CSM-level save rate: publish per-CSM save outcomes weekly starting April 21 so we can read the save mechanics.
  • Expansion was 75% of March target on the DS side — CSMs to co-build 3 expansion plays per CSM with AE partnering in April.
