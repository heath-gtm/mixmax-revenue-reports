Narrative (4 sentences)

Q1 landed $260K below Total ARR target (-2.9%), concentrated entirely in Direct Sales (-5.6%) while Self-Serve finished +0.6% to target. Two named accounts — Attentive Mobile (churn -$165K) and Meltwater (downgrade -$140K) — account for 43% of Q1 combined churn-plus-downgrade; excluding them, NRR would have been 79.6% vs the reported 56.5%. The larger structural issue is top-of-funnel: Outbound Accounts Engaged came in at 17% of target and Inbound MQLs at 56%, producing New Business bookings at 39% of plan. Q2 opens with pipeline at ~$432K future-period coverage against a target that will require an immediate SDR-capacity/motion decision and a named at-risk save plan.

Top 3 Risks

  1. Outbound TOFU collapse — Outbound pipeline at 8% of $ plan, structural. Owner: SDR + RevOps. Exposure: single largest driver of Q2 miss if unsolved.
  2. CS concentration risk — 2 accounts = 43% of Q1 churn+downgrade. Owner: CS. Action: named At-Risk Roster by Q2 W1.
  3. Bookings engine under-powered — New Business at 39% of target, all Closed Won under $20K. Owner: AE + Marketing demand. Exposure: Q2 attainment.

Top 3 Wins

  1. Self-Serve over-performed — +0.6% to target, SS Reactivation at 142%, SS Churn 15% better than plan.
  2. Expansion Contribution at 102% — the renewal-book expansion motion worked in dollars (even if Expansion bookings did not).
  3. Product PQLs at 818% of target — strong product-led signal waiting to be operationalized.

Forward-Looking Headline

We expect Q2 2026 to open with a 2x pipeline-coverage gap against target. The quarter will be won or lost on two interventions in the first 30 days: (1) fixing the Outbound SDR motion (diagnosis by W1, remediation by W3) and (2) publishing the named At-Risk Roster and executing preventive-save plays before renewal dates land. Self-Serve is projected to continue over-plan; the Meltwater re-expansion play is a known-dollar Q2 upside.
