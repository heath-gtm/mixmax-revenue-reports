The Q1 Story in Three Lines
  • Self-Serve held the line. SS ARR closed 1% above target. SS Reactivation came in 142% of target, and SS Churn actually came in better than budget (115% of plan = smaller loss than expected).
  • Direct Sales missed by $282K. The miss was not New Business alone — it was a combined Downgrade + Churn hit of $889K vs a $499K combined budget (78% over). Expansion came in at 180% of target, which partly offset the retention damage.
  • Outbound collapsed at the top of the funnel. 315 accounts engaged against an 1,890 target (17%), 5% Accounts-to-Meetings conversion, $20K of pipeline created. Inbound MQLs came in at 56%. Product was the standout — over-delivered on every top-of-funnel and conversion metric.
What Went Well
Expansion delivered 180% of target — $428,683 vs $237,802 budget. Self-Serve Reactivation 142% of target. Product channel PQLs at 832% and Product SQOs at 450% of target. These are the signals we lean into for Q2.
What Hurt
DS Churn ran 50% over budget ($386K vs $257K) and DS Downgrade ran 108% over budget ($503K vs $242K). Combined retention miss: $390K. That's the single biggest lever we'll pull in Q2 — CS playbook and account segmentation work is already in motion.
What's Next

Q2 targets land at $534K Net New ARR (vs Q1's −$373K actual) and $698K in bookings — a steep ramp, but not out of reach if we (1) fix pipeline creation (Q2 target: $2.05M, Outbound carrying $1.14M of that), (2) stabilize churn with a CS save playbook, and (3) decide whether to shift investment toward Product (our only over-performing channel) based on a Q2 mid-point diagnostic. Full Q2 plan is in the Q2 Attack Plan tab.
