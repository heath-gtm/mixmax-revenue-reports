1. Executive Summary

March closed $260K under Total ARR target ($8.68M vs $8.94M, −2.9%), with Direct Sales ARR $282K under plan and Self-Serve $22K over. The defining story is top-of-funnel collapse — DS bookings came in at 27% of target ($30K of $112K), New Business at 29%, and Outbound created only 5% of its pipeline target. CS largely held: Churn + Downgrade combined came in under budget, and Expansion + SS Reactivation over-delivered. That's why Net ARR Contribution beat target ($18K vs $6K, +301%) — retention saved March's ARR print. Read the bookings miss separately: March bookings mostly recognize into April/May ARR, not March, so the $82K bookings gap is a forward-looking ARR risk, not a March ARR hit. Q1 rollup is less forgiving: Total Net New −$373K vs −$112K target ($260K below plan), driven by DS Churn running 50% hot and DS New Business landing at 48% of target for the quarter. Rep-level: Karan 0% attainment, Isabelle 23% — both under the magnifying glass in Part 2.

Timing note — Bookings vs. ARR: Bookings (closed deals) and recognized ARR are on different clocks. A deal signed in March typically starts billing in April or later. Treat §2 as retrospective; treat §3 as the leading indicator for the next 30–60 days.
