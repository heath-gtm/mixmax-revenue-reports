Q1 2026 saw 26 accounts churn with $324K in PCV lost. The dominant theme was Competitive Loss at 61.7% of PCV ($200K), driven overwhelmingly by the $164.7K Attentive Mobile loss to Salesloft. M&A accounted for 19.2% ($62K across 5 accounts). A critical finding: the majority of high-PCV churns were deeply embedded sequence users — Attentive (25K seq emails), Instacart (39K), and Poll Everywhere (9K) all had heavy adoption before leaving. This contradicts the common assumption that churn correlates with low adoption. Instead, Q1 losses were driven primarily by executive-level decisions (new DMs, acquisitions, CRM migrations) that overrode product stickiness.

Key Takeaways

Competitive Loss dominates at 61.7% of PCV — but heavily concentrated in Attentive Mobile ($164.7K, 51% of all PCV alone). Excluding Attentive, the remaining 25 accounts represent $159.5K in PCV with a more balanced theme distribution.

M&A is the second driver at 19.2% — 5 accounts (Onfido, Ansel Health, WorkRamp, Alteia, namogoo) were acquired. These are structurally unpreventable but the pattern suggests monitoring acquisition news as an early warning signal.

Consolidation (9.7%) reveals a HubSpot pattern — Poll Everywhere, inbenta, and suitepad all consolidated into HubSpot's ecosystem. The CRM migration trend is a systemic competitive threat.

Edge case: eginnovations ($5K) — classified as "Lack of Adoption" via the Non-Responsive Win/Loss reason, but was the heaviest sequence user in the entire cohort (61K seq emails). The CTO cancelled without explanation despite active usage, illustrating that decision-maker relationships matter regardless of adoption depth.

Recommendations

1. Implement Usage-Based Churn Early Warning System

Owner: RevOps + CS Leadership  |  Impact: Could have flagged 8+ accounts months before churn

Multiple accounts (Kluster AI, eginnovations, WorkRamp) showed usage cliff-drops months before the close date with no automated alerting. Build a system that triggers CSM outreach when: (a) sequence email volume drops >40% month-over-month, (b) MAU drops >30%, or (c) an active account goes silent for 30+ days. The eginnovations case ($5K, 61K seq emails) is the strongest argument — the heaviest sequence user in the cohort cancelled with zero warning because no one was monitoring the relationship.

2. Develop a Competitive Displacement Playbook for Salesloft

Owner: Sales Leadership + Product Marketing  |  Impact: $178K PCV lost to Salesloft in Q1 alone

Two of the three largest losses (Attentive $165K, Instacart $14K) went to Salesloft. Both were heavy Mixmax users with deep sequence adoption. The losses were driven by executive-level preferences, not product shortcomings. A competitive displacement defense program — triggered when Salesloft is detected in the account — should include executive engagement, ROI documentation, and migration friction analysis.

3. Proactive HubSpot Migration Support

Owner: Product + CS  |  Impact: $24K PCV lost to HubSpot consolidation, systemic trend

Three accounts (Poll Everywhere, inbenta, suitepad) consolidated into HubSpot's ecosystem. The CRM migration from Salesforce to HubSpot is a growing industry trend. When a customer announces a CRM migration, immediately activate a retention play: proactive HubSpot integration onboarding, demonstration of Mixmax's unique value on top of HubSpot, and executive-level engagement to prevent "consolidate everything" thinking.

4. Flexible Pricing for High-Usage Self-Serve Accounts

Owner: Product + Pricing  |  Impact: $13K PCV in Self-Serve/MtM churn, some with extremely heavy usage

Cynet Locums generated 86K sequence emails over 6 months but churned over billing flexibility. CareerBeacon had 12.5K sequence emails. These accounts demonstrate clear product value but resist annual commitments. A quarterly billing option or usage-based pricing tier could retain revenue from accounts that are allergic to annual contracts.

5. M&A Monitoring and Rapid Response

Owner: CS Leadership  |  Impact: $62K PCV (5 accounts) lost to M&A

Five accounts churned due to acquisitions. While structurally difficult to prevent, earlier detection (monitoring acquisition news, LinkedIn role changes) combined with rapid outreach to the acquiring company's IT/Sales leadership could salvage some relationships. The Onfido case ($37.4K) shows that phantom MAU can mask an already-churned account — usage monitoring would have revealed the zero-engagement reality months earlier.
