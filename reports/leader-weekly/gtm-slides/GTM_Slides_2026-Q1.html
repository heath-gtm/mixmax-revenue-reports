5b · Rebrand Inflection — November 2025 rebrand becomes the Q2 narrative wedge.
Q1 2026 = first full quarter post-rebrand · strategic inflection framing · no quantitative attribution
The rebrand is strategic context — it is NOT used to explain the Q1 miss. Q2 is where brand shows up in the funnel; trendline readability arrives Q3 2026 (two quarters post-launch).
  • Q1 2026 is the first full post-rebrand quarter. Brand launched November 2025 — Q1 is the first clean commercial period inside the new identity. Not yet a trend; a baseline.
  • No quantitative attribution. Bootstrap mode means no Q4 2025 or Q1 2025 comparables exist in Gen 1. We cannot claim the rebrand caused or prevented anything measurable at this depth. Anyone who does is overreaching.
  • Self-serve surface held post-rebrand: Product PQLs 818%, SS Reactivation 142%, SS Upgrade 112%. Qualitatively consistent with a brand that resonates with existing-user self-serve demand.
  • The Q1 miss is concentration + Outbound TOFU — not brand. Two named accounts = 43% of churn+downgrade. Outbound Accounts Engaged at 17% = SDR capacity, not messaging.
  • Q2 uses the rebrand as a commercial wedge — Outbound re-launch under new positioning, Expansion narrative refresh (Tegus +71% template), Meltwater re-expansion pitch built on new value story.
  • Measurement readiness: Two quarters post-rebrand (Q3 2026) is the earliest honest window to read brand-on-funnel impact. Track MQL quality, Inbound win rate, and Expansion motion uptake as directional leading indicators in Q2.
Writing guidance: Do NOT say "the rebrand drove X." Say "Q1 is our first quarter in the new brand system — here's what held, here's what broke, and here's how Q2 uses brand as a wedge." The rebrand is an inflection-point framing, not a causal variable. Expect one board member to push for attribution — point them to the two-quarters readiness window.
