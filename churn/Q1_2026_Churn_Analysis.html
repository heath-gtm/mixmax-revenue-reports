Mixmax Q1 2026 Direct Sales Churn & Downgrade Analysis

Period: Q1 2026 (Jan – Mar) Accounts Churned: 24 PCV at Risk: $314,150 ARR Churn: $229,248 Report Date: April 15, 2026 Audience: Sales + CS Leadership

Executive Summary

Q1 2026 saw 24 accounts churn totaling $314,150 in previous contract value (PCV) and $229,248 in ARR churn. This was a significant increase driven primarily by one outsized loss: Attentive Mobile ($164,724 PCV), which selected a competitor despite heavy product usage across 345 active users with stable and growing engagement. Separately, five accounts ($62,112 combined) churned due to acquisitions — entirely unpreventable. Three self-serve/month-to-month accounts ($13,260) churned despite active or growing usage, exposing a pricing and packaging vulnerability in the current retention model.

A critical insight from this quarter: 73% of managed PCV lost came from accounts with STABLE or GROWING usage — these were business and procurement decisions, not product failures. The data shows that competitive positioning, CRM migration, and contract inflexibility were the dominant churn drivers for high-value accounts, while product quality remained strong. Only 1 of 24 accounts cited product gaps as their primary reason for leaving.

This quarter followed the new pricing and packaging launch. The data suggests that contract flexibility and competitive positioning were the primary drivers of churn, not price sensitivity or product quality. Three competitive losses accounted for $177,204 (56% of total PCV), and the Instacart deep dive revealed specific friction points: annual-only contracts when the customer wanted a 3-month extension, and monthly plan sync caps that made downgrade paths unworkable. Addressing these structural gaps should be the top priority heading into Q2.

Feature Adoption at Churn

  • 11 of 18 non-managed accounts (61%) never activated sequences — the highest-value feature was never discovered by the majority of self-serve accounts.
  • Email tracking was the stickiest but lowest-value feature. It was the only feature used by several accounts (theajp.org, Alteia) but doesn’t drive enough value to prevent churn on its own.
  • Accounts using 3+ features had higher PCV but still churned due to competitive and business reasons (Attentive, Poll Everywhere, sumup). Multi-feature adoption does not immunize against procurement-driven churn.
  • Calendar adoption appeared only in accounts that were actively evaluating (sumup: 35K events) or growing (CareerBeacon: 0 to 1,660). Calendar is an indicator of engagement depth but not retention.

Pricing & Packaging Impact

  • 3 self-serve/MtM churns ($13,260 PCV) — all had active or growing usage. MtM billing created zero friction to leave, even for accounts that were clearly getting value from the product.
  • Contract flexibility cited in Instacart meetings: Customer wanted a 3-month extension; the annual-only requirement forced a binary renew-or-churn decision. Finance was enforcing vendor reviews and needed flexibility.
  • Monthly plan sync caps made the downgrade path from annual to monthly unworkable for Instacart, removing the middle ground between full renewal and churn.
  • Post-pricing launch competitive losses: 3 accounts totaling $177K suggest a positioning gap, not a price gap. Attentive ($165K) had heavy usage and still chose a competitor — this was about competitive positioning, not value-for-money.

Recommendations

  1. Implement automated usage-decline alerts (Amplitude) for accounts with >$5K ARR. The Onfido and eginnovations patterns showed clear usage cliffs weeks before churn. An automated alert on 30%+ MAU or event decline would have surfaced these accounts for intervention.
  2. Establish an exit interview protocol for all churning accounts (managed and non-managed). Only 8% of churning accounts had any meeting trail. Even a 15-minute exit call would provide intelligence to prevent future losses in similar segments.
  3. Address the MtM/self-serve retention gap. Three active-usage accounts ($13,260) walked because MtM billing created zero friction. Consider: annual commitment incentives, usage-triggered outreach to convert MtM to annual, or a “save” flow for MtM cancellations.
  4. Require Mixmax Copilot on all CSM customer meetings. The meeting coverage gap is severe — a $165K account had zero documented meetings. Meeting recording should be mandatory for all managed accounts above a threshold.
  5. Evaluate contract flexibility options (bridge plans, short-term extensions, quarterly billing). Instacart specifically asked for a 3-month extension and was denied. A bridge plan could have preserved the relationship while the internal vendor review concluded.
  6. Sequence activation onboarding for non-managed accounts. 61% of non-managed accounts never activated a sequence. An automated onboarding flow that guides self-serve users through sequence setup in their first 14 days could significantly improve adoption and stickiness.
  7. Competitive win-back playbook for evaluation losses. The sumup and suitepad pattern (heavy evaluation followed by return to incumbent) suggests a structured win-back play at 90 days post-churn could recapture accounts where the incumbent fails to deliver.
