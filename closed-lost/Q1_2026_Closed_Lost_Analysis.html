Deals Lost to HubSpot

Q1 2026 closed 18 deals lost for $133,125 ARR (16 Net New, 1 Expansion, 1 Conversion). The dominant theme was competitive displacement: 55% of ARR went to named competitors, with HubSpot Sales Hub the #1 competitor by deal count (4 deals, $22,200) and SFDC Inbox the #1 by ARR in a single $26K loss to Haymarket Media. Only 31% of losses were truly outside our control; 69% ($91K) were preventable or possibly preventable — meaning better qualification, trial activation, or renewal motion could have changed the outcome. The expansion loss (SumUp, $9,825) is the most damning: usage declined 35% from its December peak and no save motion was captured, pointing to a CS blind spot. Scalar.io's $1,500 walk-away is a packaging failure — a healthy 23 MAU customer with no right-sized paid tier after the Growth plan was retired.

If You Read Nothing Else — 5 Things

  1. HubSpot Sales Hub is the #1 loss-to-competitor by deal count (4 deals / $22,200 ARR): Kombo, Orca AI, Strongmetrics, BLAZE. All 4 lost on stack-consolidation economics ("already pay for HubSpot, can't justify adding Mixmax"). This is a battlecard problem, not a product problem.
  2. SumUp's non-renewal ($9,825) was foreshadowed by Amplitude for 60 days — MAU peaked at 34 in December and dropped to 22 by January (a 35% decline) with zero integration depth (no SF/HubSpot events). No QBR, no save touchpoint was recorded. The canary fired and nobody picked up the phone.
  3. Discovery coverage is a gap: 11 of 18 lost deals (61%) have zero captured Mixmax meeting transcripts. Morgan Wible's inbound motion skews async/email, which hides objections and blocks coaching. The Innovamat deal closed lost "self-serve" while Amplitude showed 10 MAU actively ramping — sales never engaged.
  4. Keychain + Front are product-gap tells that repeat — Keychain asked for A/B testing + integrated data (sequence analytics), Front is a structural ICP mismatch (Mixmax is Gmail-native; Front SDRs live inside Front's client). The Keychain signal (A/B sequencing) is the product-gap we should triage with product for Q2.
  5. Trial-to-close latency is killing us. BLAZE's trial only activated 3 days before close. Haymarket had one calendar touch in July and closed lost in January — no trial, no activation, just drift. Every lost deal with a captured meeting but zero Amplitude activity (Haymarket, WellHive, BrainsWay, Keychain) points to the same fix: trial activation inside 5 business days or the deal is dead.

Lessons Learned — Keep / Stop / Start

Actionable patterns from Q1 2026 losses. Focus is on what we control.

Keep

  • Rich inbound discovery. When deals did capture meetings (WellHive 4, Triple Whale 2, BLAZE 2, Keychain 1), the transcripts were high-signal — we know exactly what the objections were.
  • Product-led signal. PLG sourcing (Triple Whale, Strongmetrics, Ballast) surfaces real intent and usable usage data. Keep mining Amplitude for PQL triggers.
  • Honest loss labeling. The AE write-ups in Win/Loss Details are candid (Jack-era flags, unresponsive admissions, "should have disqualified"). Don't lose that rigor.

Stop

  • Stop carrying deals without activation. 12 of 18 lost deals had zero MAU. A trial that hasn't activated within 5 business days is a lost cause; close it out or escalate.
  • Stop ignoring expansion canaries. SumUp declined 35% MoM for 60 days with no save motion. Never again — any Moderate+ account with >20% MAU drop triggers a required save call.
  • Stop keeping Jack-era legacy deals. SpryPoint, Triple Whale, and Haymarket all carried pre-transition baggage. Zero-activity opportunities >90 days should auto-close.
  • Stop prospecting non-Gmail clients. Front is a structural no. Add to outbound do-not-prospect list.

Start

  • Start a HubSpot co-existence battlecard. 4 losses to HubSpot SH ($22K). Build the pitch, ROI calculator, and named-win case study.
  • Start a CSM canary dashboard. MAU decline + 0-integration-depth accounts must surface in the CS book at least 90 days before renewal.
  • Start a PQL-to-AE handoff for ramping self-serve. Innovamat was 10 MAU ramping and we never engaged. Any self-serve trial hitting 5 weekly actives or 3 email senders gets an AE touchpoint.
  • Start an "activation clock." Every Mixmax Trial meeting = trial must stand up within 5 business days or the deal is escalated / closed out. No more 6-month zombie pipelines (Haymarket).
  • Start a product roadmap input for A/B sequence testing. Keychain's product-gap callout (A/B testing + integrated data) keeps surfacing. Prioritize discovery on this gap for Q3.
